# Design Context

## Target Audience

Publicly shared — used by the creator daily and occasionally by colleagues, friends, or anyone who finds it. Some users may be unfamiliar with Numbat. The interface must feel approachable and self-explanatory without being condescending to power users. Used across desktop (focused calculation sessions) and mobile (quick lookups and unit conversions on the go).

## Use Cases

Quick unit conversions, arithmetic with units, defining variables in a session, referencing results for follow-up calculations. Also used by people exploring Numbat for the first time — discoverability matters.

## Brand Personality

**Three words:** Precise. Handmade. Mineral.

The name "Calcite" is a crystalline mineral found in limestone. The brand should feel like a quality scientific field instrument or a well-made notebook: functional and serious, but warm and considered. Not a cold developer tool, not a playful consumer app — something a thoughtful person would reach for and keep.

## Tone

Warm utility. Considered rather than flashy. The aesthetic should feel built to last — like something crafted with care rather than shipped quickly.

## Reference Colors (from icon.svg)

- Top face: `#f6f1e6` — warm cream/linen (`oklch(95% 0.025 80)`)
- Right face: `#d9cfb8` — warm tan/wheat (`oklch(85% 0.030 75)`)
- Shadow face: `#7a7466` — warm brown/slate (`oklch(52% 0.025 70)`)

## Theme

Both light and dark equally important — follow `prefers-color-scheme`. Light: warm linen surfaces. Dark: deep granite with warm tints. Neither is an afterthought.

## Responsive Priority

Desktop and mobile both equally important — no compromise on either. Mobile is not a degraded desktop; each context should get the right layout for how it is actually used.

## Design Principles

1. **Crafted precision** — every spacing decision, border, and label feels deliberate; nothing is accidental
2. **Warm approachability** — new users can orient themselves quickly; the interface teaches without tutorials
3. **Typography as structure** — clear hierarchy between expression input, calculated output, labels, and chrome; the monospace output is the star
4. **Full-fidelity on all surfaces** — mobile gets the right layout for its context, not a shrunken desktop
5. **Mineral identity** — the calcite/stone metaphor (warm neutrals, crystalline geometry, amber accent) runs consistently through colour, spacing, and detail

## Anti-references

Nothing that looks like a generic SaaS product. No blue accent palettes. No purple gradients. No glowing neon on dark. No cards-everywhere layout. Should feel like handmade precision instruments, not a startup app.
